Sahil Garg

I'm a PhD Scholar in the Intelligent Systems Division (ISD) of the Department of Computer Science within the Viterbi School of Engineering at the University of Southern California (USC).

My research interest is in data driven modeling of complex systems. Along these lines, in the past, I built machine learning algorithms for problems specific to robotics, environmental phenomena, network science, natural language processing, Cancerous biopathways.

I am very fortunate to have my PhD thesis adviser Prof. Aram Galstyan. Also, guided by Prof. Greg Ver Steeg and Prof. Daniel Marcu from time to time. Our main focus is to model the semantic representation of languages (AMR) using kernel functions, for structured inference problems.

I am also collaborating with Dr. Irina Rish and Dr. Guillermo Ceechi, and many other brilliant folks in the IBM T. J. Watson Research Center. We are building representation learning algorithms, such as based on dictionary learning, inspired from the brain, for real world problems. Another interesting venture is computational psychiatry.

In the past, I had privilege of learning under guidance of Prof. Nora Ayanian, Prof. Amarjeet Singh, Prof. Fabio Ramos, and Commdel co-founders Nitin Gupta and Amit Verma. I also had a luck of working with co-founders of Snowpal Software Services, Krish Palaniappan and Harman Sahni, in its first couple of years as the sole developer (product development in education domain).
